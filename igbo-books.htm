Akuko Ifo Umuaka: 20 Short African Folktales This book “Akuko Ifo Umuaka” is a collection of 20 African folktales retold by Chisom Ekomaru and Chikamgaefe Ahumibe. Each story is written to captivate and intrigue young readers, teaching them about good morals and life lessons with a context of the African Culture. Each story includes the English and Igbo versions of the story, allowing the book to serve as a tool for users to practise reading in and listening to the Igbo language. Surviving in Biafra The book is based on history of the Nigerian Civil War. Written by Alfred Obiora Uzokwe, book was published in January 2003 and is widely available. The book explains several waves of rioting in the northern Nigeria that led to the brutal massacre of thousands of easterners living in the northern Nigerian. Easterners living in the north, by noticing that their safety could no longer be guaranteed, fled from the north to the eastern region. They started negotiations on how to live in harmony but when negotiations fail, they revert back to their original nation before British establish Nigeria. The book will be a valuable possession to own, part of history to be acquainted with, especially when children question about the war. This book can be ordered from your local bookstore or from Amazon, eBay or Wob website.

There's Rice At Home Emeka and Ogechi go to the market with their mum to buy groceries for their house. On their way home they pass by different food stalls and ask their mum if they're allowed to have something to eat even though there is rice at home. There is this notion that children like different things all the time. Children are taken to the sweet shop and asked to choose sweets of their choice, they will always select difference sweets from the one they decided on previously. The same as rice mother cooked, even though there is rice at home, children want to eat different rice from the one mummy cooked but mummy remained them that there is rice at home. This book can be bought from your local bookstore, Amazon or by searching the internet.
